Travel Concept App

PROJECT OVERVIEW


Name

Daniel Venter

Ryan Lamb

The Problem

It was made aware that it becomes overwhelming and stressful when planning out travel for modern travelers. Not knowing where to start, modern travelers would love assistance overcoming the hurdles with travel planning by having a clear travel planning pathway.

Software

InVision, Figma, Adobe CC, Miro, Zoom



Role

UX Researcher

The Solution

With research, concepts, and iterations, an app was created with step-by-step guided features to help bridge the hurdles of travel planning in regard to budgeting, reservations, and the booking process.

Tools

User Persona, Affinity Diagrams, Empathy Maps.

Research


A target audience was identified, labeled “Modern Traveler.” A proto-persona was then created based on the “Modern Traveler” audience with hypotheses on potential pain points they might endure during their travel planning process. Five users based on 20 to 40 years of age and travel frequency were identified to conduct a planned survey interviewed and recorded over Zoom. Questions were framed to look into what excites the users and what their perfect travel experiences will look like. But also how they go about their travel planning, how long it takes, and what they dislike about the planning process. Key findings and observations were that there was a high level of stress with travel planning and that there was great difficulty traveling in a group. Products in the market today that solve these hurdles, for example, are TripIt and TripAdvisor. TripIt provides up-to-date travel information and declutters itineraries but to access all the features, and it needs to be with a paid subscription. TripAdvisor has a wide network base with many reviews that informs and helps travelers with their travel planning. Additional products in the market that specialize in different areas are Hopper and Airbnb. Hopper offers up to 40% savings on flights through the analysis of billions of flight prices daily. Airbnb gives travelers the ability to rent homes instead of hotels, giving travelers the amenities of home

Interview Plan


In the past years, there has been a lot of focus on travel that has been shared through social media platforms. Modern travelers that follow these feeds want to travel more but are always looking for tips on how to do so. But with the vast amount of travel websites and platforms out today, it can get confusing. There is a need to fill this gap by providing reliable travel tips, simplifying travel planning processes, and recommendations that can serve as your own personal online travel guide.

Goals:


To develop an app that makes travel planning and arrangements easy, and that serves as a personal travel guide before and during travel with the ability to have social media attributes.

Methodology:


To obtain qualitative data, we will conduct interviews with participants. Questions will be asked to the participants from one-on-one interviews from an interview script that was planned. An online survey would also be conducted to obtain quantitative data. From the qualitative and quantitative data, a user persona will be created and be used for the development of the app.

Interesting pain points observations from the Affinity Diagram. Some of the users experienced a need to help plan travel as some don’t know how or they might feel overwhelmed. Some find it difficult to travel as a group.

User Persona


The User Persona was created from the Proto-Persona and labeled as "Sarah Yoder." Sarah has an English major with a minor in communications. She is an editor for a local newspaper and loves coffee, going away with her friends visiting travel destinations. Her goal is to visit Bora Bora in the French Polynesia Islands, publish a book about her travels and save money for a home. There are a few things that limit Sarah, and she does get overwhelmed at times, not knowing what to do, hates wasting money, and doesn't like to be late.

Competitor Analysis


Tripadvisor’s most significant advantage is the network effect. Because of the many reviews on their app, hotels, and restaurants sees this as a valuable asset. The more users are on the app, the more hotels and restaurants want to list on Tripadvisor. Users, in turn, like this because of the reviews and options.

Feature Analysis



• Access millions of reviews of hotels, flights, vacation rentals, excursions.
• Planning tool that helps to organize and see all on a map together.
• Provides great deals comparing the lower prices.
• Has the largest collection of tour s and attractions
• Modify or cancel most tour, attraction for free
• Access mobile tickets for booked tours and activities
• Use Apple Pay or PayPal to make tour or activity bookings
• Compare airfares and find great deals on flights
• Give your own reviews and photos of places you’ve visited
• View traveler and local recommended places on a map while on your trip

User Flow


I developed a user flow from the competitor analysis to find the best way for users to onboard and start their travel planning process.

Digital Wireframes


Digital wireframes were created from the user flow to be tested on test users. Observations were:

• Splitting the bill feature needed to be more visible.
• There was also the request for an area to add group traveler’s information.
• There was that concern that Check Out was not as secure.
• The profile section was confusing, and there was a feeling it serves no purpose.
• There was some confusion with the Record Keeper feature.
